# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A broad mix of practicing product managers sharpening their instincts, aspiring PMs preparing for high-stakes decisions, and fans of Lenny's Podcast who want to engage more deeply with the content. They use the app on their own time, in a focused mindset, to test their judgment against people who've actually lived the decisions in question.

## Product Purpose

The Lenny Tank is a Shark-Tank-style PM scenario practice tool. Users pick a scenario from a topic bucket, choose their move (or write their own), and get candid feedback from Lenny's Podcast guest "judges." The product exists to make the gap between reading about product thinking and actually practicing it much smaller. Success looks like a user walking away with a sharper sense of their instincts and where they diverge from the guests' hard-won perspective.

## Brand Personality

Warm, sharp, and credible. Confident without posturing. The product should feel like stepping into a room with smart people who give you honest, direct feedback — not a game and not a lecture. The orange brand is energizing, not cute. The dark result screen is deliberate: you are being judged, and that has weight.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS / startup cream: neutral beige surfaces, Inter font, softly rounded corners, standard Tailwind component libraries. The product already diverges here and should stay that way.
- LinkedIn / corporate serious: stiff, credential-heavy, over-professional. This is practice, not a performance review.
- Gamified / badge-heavy UIs: streaks, XP bars, achievement unlocks. The stakes should feel real, not like a language app.

## Design Principles

1. **Credibility through specificity.** The judges are real people with real opinions. The UI should honor that weight: their names, their episode context, their actual words. Nothing should feel generic or interchangeable.
2. **Stakes feel real.** This is practice, but it shouldn't feel safe. The intentional shift to a dark, pressured result screen is the right call. Downstream pages should build toward that moment, not diffuse it.
3. **Warmth without softness.** The orange brand is a commitment, not decoration. Use it with intention. Don't drift toward friendly-generic by adding rounded blobs, pastel tints, or illustrated mascots.
4. **The medium is the message.** A scenario practice tool for product thinkers should itself demonstrate good product thinking: clear information hierarchy, no wasted chrome, decisions that have a reason behind them.
5. **Serve the workflow.** Downstream pages exist to move the user efficiently into a scenario and out with feedback. Design should accelerate that, never compete with the content.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance as baseline. Ensure sufficient contrast on both the warm light surfaces and the dark result page (the dark theme's secondary text is at risk). No known special accommodations required, but reduced-motion preference should be respected for the result page animations.
